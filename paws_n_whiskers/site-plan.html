Overview

Purpose

The main purpose is to connect displaced animals with responsible and nurturing families or individuals for the purpose of adoption. The secondary purpose is to inform current and potential owners about pet care.

Audience

The target audience is tech-friendly people of all ages who are seeking the companionship of a pet, or who would like tips on pet care.

Dynamic elements

1. When the home page loads, a random animal that is currently up for adoption will dynamically be inserted into the main page, with its photo and accompanying details.

2. There will be a search box to allow users to search for their animal of choice and have the results dynamically displayed on the page.

3. On the child page, in the adoption application, there will be an additional question dynamically inserted into the form, which is dependent upon the user's response to the previous question.

4. When the application confirmation is returned, it will be personalized with the applicant's name, the total number of members in their household and their pet of choice.

Page 2 will list these considerations for the applicant before adopting: 1. Pets are a lifetime committment; most cats/dogs/rabbits live 10-16 years. Are you ready? 2. Does your household have children or elderly members that your pet needs to get along with? 3. Are you ready to deal with pet hair on a daily basis? There will be shedding. 4. Is anyone in your household allergic to animals? 5. Are you ready for the financial committment? Pets need food, grooming, and vet care. 6. Who will carry the responsibility for your pet's care? Will they be home during the day? 7. Do you have the equipment? All adopted cats/rabbits must be transported home in a secure carrier. Dogs must be leashed and collared.

This is the information that will appear in the Adoption Fees section: The adoption fee includes up-to-date vaccinations, micro-chipping, flea/worm treatments, and spaying/neutering. Fees: Cats-$200; Dogs-$450; Rabbits-$100; Senior Animals (6 yrs+): Cats-$75; Senior Dogs-$150; Senior Rabbits-$35;

The application form will ask for these pieces of information: first name, last name, email, phone number, address, if they live in a house or apartment (dynamically inserted questions here: if house-do they have a fenced yard, if apt.-Do they have their landlord's approval), number of children in the household, number of adults in the household, occupation, type of pet they want, and if there are other pets in the household.

I couldn't fit all the questions on the child page wireframe, so you can't see what the the questions are that will trigger the dynamic content. The user will be asked if they live in an apartment or house, that is where another question will be dynamically inserted depending on their answer.

Also, one of the questions on the application is the number of children and the number of adults living in the house. Those two inputs will be retrieved and added together using the reduce() method and then the total will be dynamically inserted into the confirmation for the form submission.
